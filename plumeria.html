Plumeria — commonly called Frangipani — is one of the most iconic tropical flowering plants on earth. Known for its distinctive five-petalled blooms arranged in elegant clusters at branch tips, it is synonymous with warm-climate beauty. The flowers carry one of the most intoxicating fragrances in the plant kingdom — sweet, rich, and unmistakably tropical. Revered across South and Southeast Asia, it adorns temples, gardens, and hair alike. Originally from Central America and the Caribbean, Plumeria is now beloved worldwide. We offer two timeless varieties: the warmly romantic Pink and the pristine, luminous White.

Fragrance Peaks at Dusk
Plumeria releases its strongest fragrance in the late evening — the scent is produced to attract night-flying moths for pollination. Planting near a bedroom window or evening seating area transforms warm nights into a truly sensory experience.

Why do I matter?
My Superpowers
Iconic Fragrance
Among the most intensely fragrant flowers on earth — the scent of Plumeria defines tropical evenings and is used in world-class perfumery.
Thrives in Indian Heat
Plumeria loves intense sun and dry spells — the very conditions that challenge most ornamental plants are where Plumeria truly excels.
Elegant Branch Structure
Even without flowers, Plumeria's sculptural branching pattern creates a striking silhouette — beautiful year-round, bare or blooming.
Sacred & Symbolic
Deeply revered across Hindu, Buddhist, and Balinese traditions — Plumeria flowers are offered at temples daily and symbolise devotion and new beginnings.
Great in Large Containers
With the right pot and pruning, Plumeria grows beautifully in containers — ideal for terraces and courtyard gardens where its scent can be fully appreciated.
Low Maintenance
Once established, Plumeria asks for very little — minimal watering, occasional feeding, and an annual prune in February. Exceptionally low-effort for such spectacular reward.
Psst — secrets
Did You Know?
No Nectar — A Beautiful Lie
Plumeria produces no nectar whatsoever. Its fragrance mimics nectar-producing flowers to fool night moths into pollinating it — one of nature's most elegant deceptions.
Named After a French Botanist
Plumeria is named after Charles Plumier, a 17th-century French botanist who documented it during explorations in the Americas. The plant has carried his name ever since.
Hawaii's Beloved Lei Flower
Plumeria is the most iconic flower used in Hawaiian leis — the pink and white varieties are the most prized for their beauty and enduring fragrance.
Grows from Cuttings Easily
Plumeria propagates effortlessly from stem cuttings — simply let a cut dry for a week, then plant in gritty soil. New roots form within weeks, making it one of the easiest plants to share and multiply.
