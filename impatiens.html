Impatiens — affectionately called Busy Lizzie — is the undisputed champion of the shade garden. Loved for its ability to bloom relentlessly in low-light conditions where most flowering plants refuse to perform, it produces cheerful, flat-faced flowers in a vivid range of colours from fiery orange to pure white. Originally from East Africa and Asia, Impatiens fills dark corners, hanging baskets, and shaded balconies with continuous, carefree colour across all five varieties.

Self-Cleaning — No Deadheading Needed
Unlike most flowering plants, Impatiens is entirely self-cleaning — spent flowers drop off on their own and new buds immediately replace them. This means you get a perpetually tidy, continuously blooming plant with almost zero maintenance effort.

Why do I matter?
My Superpowers
Blooms in Deep Shade
The only popular flowering plant that produces vibrant, prolific blooms in full shade — perfect for north-facing balconies and shaded gardens.
Truly Self-Cleaning
Spent blooms drop themselves — no deadheading required. Impatiens is the most maintenance-free flowering plant for busy gardeners.
Non-Stop Year-Round Colour
Impatiens flowers every single month of the year in warm climates — providing uninterrupted colour even through cooler winter months.
Ideal for Hanging Baskets
Impatiens' mounding, cascading growth habit makes it the perfect choice for hanging baskets, window boxes, and trailing container displays.
5 Cheerful Colours
Orange, Pink, Light Purple, Red, and White — a complete palette for mixing and matching across containers, beds, and borders.
Fast & Forgiving
Impatiens recovers quickly from wilting, establishes fast from transplant, and forgives irregular watering better than most shade plants.
Psst — secrets
Did You Know?
The Name Means "Impatient"
Impatiens gets its name from the explosive way its seed pods burst open when touched — the seeds are "impatiently" ejected up to several feet away, a clever self-dispersal mechanism.
Over 1,000 Species
Impatiens is one of the largest genera of flowering plants with over 1,000 known species — more than almost any other genus, found across Asia, Africa, and the Americas.
World's Best-Selling Annual
Impatiens has been the best-selling annual bedding plant in the world for decades — hundreds of millions of plants are sold globally each year, more than any other flowering annual.
Used in Traditional Medicine
In Asian traditional medicine, Impatiens balsamina has been used for centuries to treat burns, warts, and inflammation — and is still studied today for its antimicrobial compounds.
Flowers Without Petals
Botanically, what appear to be Impatiens petals are actually fused sepals and petals — a unique floral structure called "zygomorphic" that evolved to precisely guide specific pollinators.
