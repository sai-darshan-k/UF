Herbaceous Perennial Mediterranean Origin Fragrant Bloom Ruffled Petals 5 Varieties

Carnation is one of the world's oldest cultivated flowers, grown for over 2,000 years across Mediterranean civilisations. The name derives from the Latin "Dianthus" — "divine flower" — and it has been used in art, culture, and ceremony across generations. Known for their intensely ruffled petals and rich clove-like fragrance, Carnations are among the most long-lasting cut flowers. In the garden, they bloom prolifically in cool weather and reward regular deadheading with wave after wave of blossoms. Available in five varieties — from compact dwarf forms to classic full-height plants.

Deadhead for Endless Blooms
The secret to non-stop Carnation flowering is simple — remove spent blooms the moment they fade. This prevents seed formation and redirects all the plant's energy into producing the next flush of flowers, resulting in continuous colour through the entire cool season.

Why do I matter?
My Superpowers
Rich Clove Fragrance
Carnations carry one of the most distinctive and beloved fragrances in the flower world — a warm, spicy clove scent that perfumes the entire garden and home.
3-Week Vase Life
As a cut flower, Carnation outlasts almost every other bloom — lasting up to 3 weeks in a vase with proper care, making them outstanding value for arrangements.
Cool Season Powerhouse
While most flowering plants struggle in winter, Carnations thrive — making them essential for colour and fragrance through the Indian cool season from November to March.

Attracts Butterflies

Did You Know?
The Divine Flower
The name "Dianthus" comes from the Greek words dios (divine) and anthos (flower) — literally the "flower of the gods." Ancient Greeks and Romans used Carnations in ceremonial garlands and festive wreaths to honour deities and celebrate victories.
2,000 Years of Cultivation
Carnations are among the oldest cultivated flowers in human history, with documented cultivation stretching back over 2,000 years. They appear in Renaissance paintings, medieval poetry, and ancient Greek texts — a flower that has never gone out of fashion.
Colour Carries Meaning
Carnation colours carry distinct traditional meanings — pink for a mother's love, red for deep admiration, white for pure love and luck, and dark pink for gratitude. This symbolism made Carnations central to Mother's Day traditions across the world.
The World's Top Cut Flower
Carnations are consistently ranked among the top three most traded cut flowers globally, alongside roses and chrysanthemums. Colombia alone exports billions of Carnation stems annually to markets across North America and Europe.
Edible and Medicinal
Carnation petals are edible and have been used in cooking and herbal medicine for centuries. They were used to flavour wine and beer in Elizabethan England and are still used today as an edible garnish. Traditional medicine systems use Carnation extracts as a mild nervine tonic.
