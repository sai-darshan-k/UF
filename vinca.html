Vinca — also known as Periwinkle or Sadabahar — is India's most beloved summer flowering plant and for very good reason. Originally from Madagascar, this heat-defying champion produces an unbroken cascade of cheerful five-petalled flowers from spring through the hottest months of summer, thriving precisely where most flowering plants surrender. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and its botanical name Catharanthus means "pure flower" — a fitting name for blooms of such unspoilt, radiant colour. Our 11 variety collection spans every shade: deep fuchsia, purple, a spectrum of pinks, sun-kissed papaya and peach, rich raspberry, vibrant red, and clean whites — many with striking contrasting centres.

Sadabahar — Always in Bloom
Its Hindi name Sadabahar literally means "always in season" — and it earns that name completely. Vinca blooms non-stop for 9–10 months of the year, producing hundreds of fresh flowers without deadheading. It is also the source of vinblastine and vincristine — powerful compounds used in cancer treatment.

Why do I matter?
My Superpowers
India's Summer Hero
When 40°C heat shuts down most flowers, Vinca blazes on. It genuinely thrives in full tropical summer sun — the toughest flowering plant for Indian gardens.
Self-Cleaning Bloomer
Unlike most flowers, Vinca drops spent blooms on its own — no deadheading needed. It continuously produces fresh flowers for months without any intervention.
Drought Warrior
Once established, Vinca tolerates irregular watering and dry spells with remarkable resilience — perfect for busy gardeners and hot balconies.
11 Colour Varieties
Solids, bicolours, eye-centred patterns — 11 varieties covering every tone from deep purple to papaya to white, ideal for mixed displays and borders.
Medicinal Legacy
Vinca is the source of vinblastine and vincristine — Nobel Prize-linked alkaloids used in chemotherapy worldwide. Beauty and science in one extraordinary plant.
Container & Bed Perfect
Compact, upright growth makes Vinca equally spectacular in pots, window boxes, garden borders, and mass plantings — a design-friendly all-rounder.
Psst — secrets
Did You Know?
Pure Flower — Catharanthus
Vinca's botanical name Catharanthus comes from the Greek for "pure flower." Its five symmetrical petals pinwheel perfectly around a contrasting centre — a design found almost nowhere else in nature at this scale.
It Saves Lives
Vinca is the natural source of vinblastine and vincristine — two alkaloids now essential in treating leukemia, Hodgkin's lymphoma, and other cancers. This humble garden flower has saved millions of lives worldwide.
Sadabahar — Always Blooming
Its Indian name Sadabahar means "forever in season" — and Vinca earns it. Unlike seasonal bloomers, it flowers from March through November in India, enduring the full force of Indian summers without pause.
No Deadheading Needed
Vinca is fully self-cleaning — spent flowers drop on their own and new blooms open immediately in their place. Hundreds of flowers can appear simultaneously on a single well-grown plant with zero maintenance.
Not the Original Vinca
Though called Vinca worldwide, Catharanthus roseus is a completely separate genus from the true Vinca (Vinca minor). They just look similar — a case of botanical mistaken identity that has stuck for centuries.
