Geranium — technically Pelargonium — is one of the most widely grown flowering plants on earth. Beloved for its cheerful rounded flower clusters, aromatic foliage, and extraordinary colour range, it thrives in pots, balconies, garden beds, and window boxes alike. Originally from South Africa, this tough yet beautiful plant blooms nearly year-round in warm climates, rewarding minimal care with generous cascades of colour. Available in ten spectacular varieties.

Deadheading Doubles Blooms
Removing spent flower heads — a practice called deadheading — tricks the Geranium into continuous blooming. The plant redirects energy from seed production into creating fresh flower clusters, resulting in up to double the number of blooms over a season.

Psst — secrets
Did You Know?
Not a True Geranium
The beloved "Geranium" of gardens is actually Pelargonium, a separate genus. The mix-up dates back to the 18th century when both were classified together — and the name stuck forever.
African Origins
Geranium originates from the Cape of Good Hope in South Africa, where over 270 species grow wild. Dutch traders introduced it to Europe in the 17th century.
The Perfume Plant
Geranium oil is one of the most important ingredients in perfumery — used as a rose substitute in countless fragrances and grown commercially across Egypt, China, and South Africa.
Natural Mosquito Repellent
Citronella Geranium varieties repel mosquitoes naturally. Growing them near windows or seating areas creates a fragrant barrier that insects strongly dislike.
Edible and Medicinal
Geranium flowers and leaves are entirely edible — used to flavour sugars, jellies, and teas. In traditional medicine, used for centuries to treat wounds and skin conditions.
